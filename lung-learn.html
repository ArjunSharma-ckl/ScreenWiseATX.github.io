Lung Cancer

LDCT screening Quitting support

Lung cancer begins in the lungs and is most common among people who smoke. Low-dose CT (LDCT) scans can catch cancer early, when it is easier to treat.

What is it?

Cancer that starts in the lung tissue or airways. Small tumors may not cause symptoms until they are advanced, which is why screening matters for people at higher risk.

Annual low-dose CT scans can lower the chance of dying from lung cancer for people who qualify.

Who is most affected?

  • Current or former smokers aged 50-80 with a 20 pack-year history
  • People who still smoke or quit within the last 15 years
  • Individuals exposed to secondhand smoke, radon, or workplace carcinogens

Risk factors

  • Cigarette, cigar, or pipe smoking
  • Radon exposure at home or work
  • Asbestos, diesel exhaust, or other workplace chemicals
  • Family history of lung cancer

Symptoms—talk to a clinician if you notice:

  • New or worsening cough, or coughing up blood
  • Shortness of breath, chest pain, or hoarseness lasting more than two weeks
  • Recurring bronchitis or pneumonia
  • Unexplained weight loss or fatigue

Prevention

  • Quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke
  • Test your home for radon and fix if high
  • Use protective equipment if exposed to dust, fumes, or chemicals
  • Stay up to date with LDCT screening if you qualify
