Colon Cancer

Educational overview based on the provided ScreenWise flyer data. Not medical advice.

Screening info Symptoms

What it is

High-level definition (educational).

Tumor growth of cancer cells in the tissue of the colon

Who could have it

Age, demographics, habits (as listed).

Who

  • Being older than 50 yrs old
  • African ethnicity
  • History of inflammatory bowel disease
  • Family history
  • Obesity
  • Smoking and drinking

Notes

Use this page to understand screening options and awareness categories.

Risk factors & symptoms

Lists below reflect flyer content.

Risk factors

  • Age (50+)
  • Family history
  • Inflammatory bowel disease
  • Obesity
  • Smoking
  • Alcohol

Symptoms

  • Change in bowel habits
  • Blood in stool
  • Abdominal discomfort
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue

Screening & access

Method, frequency, cost, insurance.

Screening method

Colonoscopy (allows doctors to see inside the colon and remove polyps)

Frequency: Every 10 years starting at age 50 (or earlier if high risk)

Cost & insurance

Cost: $1,000–$3,000 (may be higher depending on facility and sedation)

Insurance: Many insurance plans cover colonoscopy as preventive screening (varies by plan)

Clinics that accommodate

  • People's Community Clinic, Hope Clinic, and CommUnityCare offer screenings for low-income individuals
