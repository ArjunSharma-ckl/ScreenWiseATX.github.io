Prostate Cancer

Educational overview based on the provided ScreenWise flyer data. Not medical advice.

Screening info Symptoms

What it is

High-level definition (educational).

A tumor that forms in the prostate gland, right below the bladder

Who could have it

Age, demographics, habits (as listed).

Who

  • Common amongst older men above the age of 65
  • Higher risk in African American men
  • Family history

Notes

This page focuses on awareness + screening access information.

Risk factors & symptoms

Lists below reflect flyer content.

Risk factors

  • Age
  • Family history
  • Race (higher risk in African American men)

Symptoms

  • Difficulty urinating
  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Blood in urine or semen
  • Painful urination or ejaculation
  • Pain in lower back, hips, or thighs

Screening & access

Method, frequency, cost, insurance.

Screening method

PSA blood test (looks for elevated prostate-specific antigen)

Frequency: Discuss screening with a doctor starting around age 50 (earlier for higher risk)

Cost & insurance

Cost: $30–$50 (varies by lab/clinic)

Insurance: Often covered, especially for higher-risk patients (varies by plan)
