Cervical Cancer

Educational overview based on the provided ScreenWise flyer data. Not medical advice.

Screening info Symptoms

What it is

High-level definition (educational).

A type of cancer that occurs in the cells of the cervix, which is the lower part of the uterus that connects to the vagina.

Who could have it

Age, demographics, habits (as listed).

Who

  • Most common in women aged 30 to 50
  • Rare under the age of 20
  • HPV (Human papillomavirus) infection

Notes

Education and screening awareness are key themes on this page.

Risk factors & symptoms

Lists below reflect flyer content.

Risk factors

  • Persistent HPV infection
  • Smoking
  • Weakened immune system
  • Long-term use of oral contraceptives
  • Multiple pregnancies

Symptoms

  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding
  • Unusual vaginal discharge
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Pelvic pain

Screening & access

Method, frequency, cost, insurance.

Screening method

1. Pap Smear (Pap Test)
A healthcare professional collects cells from the cervix to look for abnormal changes.

2. HPV Test
Checks for HPV types that may cause cervical cancer.

Frequency: Women 21–29: Pap smear every 3 years. Women 30–65: Pap + HPV test every 5 years (or Pap alone every 3 years).

Cost & insurance

Cost: Pap smear: $80–$200. HPV test: $40–$100.

Insurance: Covered by most insurance plans; many plans cover as preventive care.
