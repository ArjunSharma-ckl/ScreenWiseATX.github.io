Cervical Cancer

Educational awareness content based on the ScreenWise flyer.

What it is

Cancer that occurs in the cells of the cervix, the lower part of the uterus connecting to the vagina.

Who could have it
  • Most common in women aged 30–50
  • Rare in women under 20
  • Associated with HPV infection
Risk factors
  • Persistent HPV infection
  • Smoking
  • Weakened immune system
  • Long-term oral contraceptive use
  • Multiple pregnancies
Symptoms
  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding
  • Unusual vaginal discharge
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Pelvic pain
Screening, frequency & cost

Pap smear and HPV test.

Pap smear every 3 years (ages 21–29).

Pap + HPV every 5 years or Pap alone every 3 years (ages 30–65).

Cost: Pap $80–$200, HPV $40–$100.

Covered by most insurance plans.
