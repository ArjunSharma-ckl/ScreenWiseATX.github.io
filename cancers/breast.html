Breast Cancer

Educational overview based on the provided ScreenWise flyer data. Not medical advice.

Screening info Symptoms

What it is

High-level definition (educational).

Tumor growth of mutated cells in the breast tissue

Who could have it

Age, demographics, habits (as listed).

Who

  • Above the age of 50
  • Any family history of breast cancer
  • More common in women

Notes

Risk can vary by genetics and lifestyle. Use this page as an awareness guide.

Risk factors & symptoms

Lists below reflect flyer content.

Risk factors

  • Overweight/Obese (BMI above 25) and being physically inactive
  • Family history of breast cancer
  • Smoking
  • Heavy alcohol intake

Symptoms

  • New lump in breast or underarm (armpit)
  • Thickening or swelling of part of the breast
  • Irritation or dimpling of breast skin
  • Redness or flaky skin in the nipple area or the breast
  • Pain in any area of the breast
  • Nipple discharge other than breast milk

Screening & access

Method, frequency, cost, insurance.

Screening method

- Mammogram (stand in front of the X-ray machine and lie on the table so that your breast is placed between two plates)

Frequency: Every 2 years for women age 50-74

Cost & insurance

Cost: Mammogram: $100 - $250 and may exceed $1,000 in some cases

Insurance: Covered by most insurances
