Breast Cancer

Educational awareness content based on the ScreenWise flyer.

What it is

Tumor growth of mutated cells in the breast tissue.

Who could have it
  • People over the age of 50
  • Individuals with family history of breast cancer
  • More common in women
Risk factors
  • Overweight or obesity (BMI above 25)
  • Physical inactivity
  • Family history
  • Smoking
  • Heavy alcohol intake
Symptoms
  • New lump in breast or underarm
  • Thickening or swelling of part of the breast
  • Irritation or dimpling of breast skin
  • Redness or flaky skin near nipple
  • Pain in any area of the breast
  • Nipple discharge other than breast milk
Screening, frequency & cost

Mammogram every 2 years for women ages 50–74.

Cost: $100–$250 (can exceed $1,000).

Covered by most insurance plans.
