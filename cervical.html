Cervical Cancer

Cervical cancer screening detects changes in cervical cells before they become cancer. Routine testing is the most reliable way to prevent invasive cervical disease.

  • Start regular screening at age 21
  • Use Pap tests, HPV tests, or both depending on age
  • Vaccination and safe practices lower risk

Pap Test

Collects cervical cells to look for precancerous changes. Recommended every 3 years for ages 21-29, and every 3 years for 30-65 if HPV test is not available.

HPV Test

Looks for high-risk human papillomavirus types that can lead to cervical cancer. Ages 30-65 can opt for HPV testing alone every 5 years.

Co-testing

Combines Pap and HPV tests during the same visit. Recommended every 5 years for ages 30-65 for the most thorough screening approach.

HPV Vaccination

The HPV vaccine protects against the virus types that cause most cervical cancers. It’s recommended between ages 9 and 26, with catch-up doses through age 45.

When to See a Doctor

  • Postpartum or post-menopausal spotting
  • Pain during sex or pelvic pain
  • Abnormal discharge or bleeding between periods
