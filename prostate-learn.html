Prostate Cancer

PSA awareness Shared decisions

Prostate cancer starts in the prostate gland. Screening with PSA blood tests and sometimes a digital rectal exam (DRE) helps catch fast-growing cancers early.

What is it?

Cancer that forms in the prostate, a small gland below the bladder. Many prostate cancers grow slowly, but some are aggressive and benefit from early detection.

Talk with your clinician about PSA/DRE benefits and timing for you.

Who is most affected?

  • Men and people assigned male at birth, especially 50+
  • Black men have higher risk and may need earlier conversations
  • Anyone with a father, brother, or son with prostate cancer

Risk factors

  • Age (risk rises after 50)
  • Family history of prostate or certain breast cancers (BRCA mutations)
  • Diet high in processed meats and low in fruits/vegetables
  • Possible higher risk with obesity or certain hormone levels

Symptoms—talk to a clinician if you notice:

  • Weak urine stream, trouble starting/stopping, or frequent nighttime urination
  • Blood in urine or semen
  • Pelvic discomfort, lower back pain, or unexplained weight loss

Prevention and monitoring

  • Discuss PSA/DRE timing based on your risk
  • Stay active, maintain a balanced diet, and avoid smoking
  • Monitor PSA trends over time; further imaging/biopsy may be advised if elevated
