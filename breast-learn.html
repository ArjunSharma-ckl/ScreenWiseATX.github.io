Breast Cancer

Early detection Breast health basics

Breast cancer happens when cells in breast tissue grow and form a tumor. Finding it early means more treatment options and higher survival.

What is it?

Breast cancer is the growth of abnormal cells that can invade nearby tissue or spread elsewhere in the body.

Regular mammograms catch most cancers before they can be felt.

Who is most affected?

  • Women 40+ (risk increases with age)
  • Anyone with family history of breast, ovarian, pancreatic, or prostate cancer
  • Higher rates in Black women at younger ages

Risk factors

  • Inherited genes (BRCA1/2, p53, CHEK2, ATM, PALB)
  • Chest radiation or hormone replacement therapy with estrogen/progesterone
  • Overweight/obesity, low physical activity, or frequent alcohol use
  • First period before 12 or menopause after 55
  • Pregnancy after age 30

Symptoms—call your doctor if you notice:

  • New lump in the breast or underarm
  • Swelling, dimpling, redness, or flaky skin on the breast
  • Nipple pulling inward, pain, or discharge (including blood)
  • Any change in size, shape, or persistent breast pain

What lowers risk

  • Staying active and maintaining a healthy weight
  • Limiting alcohol
  • Talking with your clinician about genetic testing if family history is strong
