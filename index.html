WHY IT MATTERS

Cancer screening is a method that not only allows for detection of cancer, but it also is a prevention tactic. Oftentimes, people aren't aware of the frequency at which cancer screenings should be done and miss out on the opportunity of early detection. If we can promote more awareness of early detection cancer, we can catch this disease at an earlier stage, making it more manageable for healthcare professionals to treat. Additionally, screening is often free or covered by health insurance, so taking advantage of this could change your life.

  • Cancer screening allows for early cancer detection for some common cancers. This is important because screening can eventually prevent cancer, making it a powerful tool in many adult's lives.
  • Cancer screening allows early detection of some common cancers.
  • Cancer screening can prevent cancer (e.g., by removing precancerous polyps during a colonoscopy).
  • Only 69% of people undergo cancer screening at the recommended age.
  • 14% of all cancers are caught through screening, but of those caught early, over 90% of patients fully recover (survive 5+ years).
  • Screening is often free or covered by health insurance under the Affordable Care Act and most private plans.
